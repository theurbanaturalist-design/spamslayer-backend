EXHIBIT D: Carrier Call Detail Records (CDR)

    Independent call records from Plaintiff's telephone carrier
    corroborating incoming calls from:
    +15555550188
    on the following dates: 2026-05-03, 2026-05-03.

    Carrier CDRs are the STRONGEST corroborating evidence because
    they are generated by a neutral third party (your phone company)
    with no connection to your compliance system or Twilio.

    → HOW TO GET YOUR CDRs:

      OPTION 1 — ONLINE (fastest, free):
        Most carriers let you download call history online:
        • AT&T:     att.com → My AT&T → Usage → Call History
        • T-Mobile:  t-mobile.com → Usage → Call Details
        • Verizon:  verizonwireless.com → My Usage → View Usage
        • Sprint:   sprint.com → My Sprint → My Usage
        Download or screenshot the records showing the spam calls.
        Print the pages with the matching dates highlighted.

      OPTION 2 — REQUEST FORMAL CDRs (more authoritative):
        Call your carrier's customer service and request:
        "I need a formal Call Detail Record report for my number
         +13375550100 covering 2026-05-03 through
         2026-05-03. This is for use as evidence in a
         court proceeding."
        Carriers are required to provide customers access to their
        own call records. Some carriers charge a small fee ($5-$25)
        for formal CDRs. Formal CDRs typically include: originating
        number, date, time, duration, and call type.

        ⚠ RETENTION WARNING: Most carriers retain CDRs for only
        18-24 months. If your earliest calls are older than 18
        months, request CDRs IMMEDIATELY before they are purged.
        If CDRs have already been purged, your phone bill
        screenshots and Twilio logs still serve as evidence.

      OPTION 3 — SUBPOENA DEFENDANT'S CARRIER (advanced):
        If the defendant disputes making the calls, you can
        subpoena the defendant's phone carrier to produce THEIR
        outbound call records showing calls to your number.
        Ask the clerk about issuing a subpoena duces tecum.
        This is usually only needed if the defendant shows up
        and denies making the calls.

    → WHAT THE CDR PROVES:
      • The calls actually happened (independent of your system)
      • The exact dates and times match your petition
      • The originating phone number matches the defendant
      • The call duration confirms a connection was made
      • Your carrier has no motive to fabricate records
