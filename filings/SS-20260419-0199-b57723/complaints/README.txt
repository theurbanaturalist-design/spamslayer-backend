PURPOSE

  This bundle contains DRAFTS of lawful complaints you can file against
  the offender without going to court. Every draft is based on your call
  records; every draft must be reviewed by you before submission.

  None of these complaints touch the offender's systems, send traffic to
  their endpoints, or impersonate them. Each routes through channels
  those entities are legally required to answer.

LEGAL DISCLAIMER

  Knowingly false statements to a federal agency violate 18 U.S.C. § 1001
  and carry criminal penalties. State-AG false-complaint statutes carry
  similar penalties. If you are not certain a fact stated in a draft is
  true, DELETE IT before submitting. A complaint is more effective when
  every sentence is defensible.

  SpamSlayer is not your attorney. This bundle is a drafting aid, not
  legal advice. If any complaint could implicate you criminally,
  financially, or reputationally, consult counsel before filing.

FILING ORDER (file in this order for maximum leverage)

  1. ITG Traceback Request (tracebacks.org)
     Submit: https://tracebacks.org
     Why:    Forces originating carrier to identify the downstream subscriber or face de-peering by upstream carriers. Highest operational impact — a pattern of tracebacks removes providers from the Robocall Mitigation Database, at which point US carriers are required to block all their traffic.

  2. FCC TCPA / Unwanted-Calls Complaint
     Submit: https://consumercomplaints.fcc.gov/hc/en-us/requests/new
     Why:    Feeds the FCC Enforcement Bureau's complaint queue. Volume-weighted: a single complaint is one data point; a pattern triggers 4-notice letters under the TRACED Act and can force removal from the Robocall Mitigation Database.

  3. FTC Do-Not-Call Registry Complaint
     Submit: https://www.donotcall.gov/report.html
     Why:    Feeds FTC telemarketing enforcement. Civil penalties up to $51,744 per call (2026 inflation-adjusted). High-volume operators get swept into joint FTC/state-AG actions (e.g. Operation Call It Quits).

  4. State AG Complaint — Louisiana AG — Consumer Protection (File a Complaint)
     Submit: https://www.ag.state.la.us/Complaints
     Why:    Feeds in-state civil enforcement and the NAAG multi-state telemarketing consortium. Some states (IN, MO, TX, MA, WA) are particularly aggressive on telemarketing and have obtained seven- and eight-figure judgments against VSC and solar operators. Your individual complaint adds to the pattern those actions are built on.

  5. BBB Complaint — Preferred Auto Protection [DEMO]
     Submit: https://www.bbb.org/file-a-complaint
     Why:    Reputation signal against a named, legitimate seller. Not applicable to shell operators. Some VSC and solar sellers treat BBB rating as a customer-acquisition input and will respond quickly to avoid a rating downgrade.

SKIPPED (and why)

  • cfpb:
    No financial-product pitch detected in call transcripts. CFPB jurisdiction covers debt collection, student loans, credit cards, mortgages, and related consumer-financial products only. Skipped.

HOW TO USE THIS BUNDLE

  1. Open each draft file in the order above.
  2. Read every sentence. Remove anything you cannot swear is true.
  3. Open the submission URL listed at the top of the draft.
  4. Paste the draft body (or the relevant fields) into the form.
  5. Attach call recordings if the form allows.
  6. Submit. Save your confirmation email / reference number.
  7. Log the submission so you can follow up (FCC and CFPB will
     usually respond within 15 business days; ITG sooner).

TIME BUDGET

  Realistically about 30–45 minutes for the full bundle. ITG takes the
  longest because the form is detailed; FTC DNC is the fastest. None
  costs you anything except your time.
